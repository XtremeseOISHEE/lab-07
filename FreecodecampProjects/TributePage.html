Dr. Norman Borlaug: Father of the Green Revolution

Dr. Norman Borlaug is credited with saving over a billion people from starvation through his revolutionary agricultural innovations. His work in developing high-yielding crop varieties was crucial in combating hunger in many developing countries, especially in the 1960s and 1970s.

In recognition of his contributions, he was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970. His efforts laid the foundation for the Green Revolution, which dramatically increased food production worldwide.
